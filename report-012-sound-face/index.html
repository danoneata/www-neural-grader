Clear and sound cuttings

When grading, we measure the yield based on the clear cuttings. A clear cuttings is defect-free and sound on the reverse side (that is, it doesn't contain unsound defects larger than 1/4" on the opossite side). In the initial experiments, I have treated the two sides independently (method 1). Now I'm trying to incorporate this rule by simply propagating the unsound defects from on side to the other (method 2).
